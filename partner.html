    Philippine Association of Physics and Science Instructors

    PAPSI Partner Schools Program

    Strengthening Science Education Through Institutional Collaboration
    and Teacher Professional Development

    1 Introduction / Background

    The Philippine Association of Physics and Science Instructors (PAPSI) is a professional organization dedicated to advancing the quality of physics and science education in the Philippines. Since its establishment, PAPSI has been committed to the continuous professional growth of science educators through seminars, webinars, workshops, conferences, and collaborative educational initiatives.

    The PAPSI Partner Schools Program is established to build stronger institutional partnerships with schools that actively support the professional growth of science educators through PAPSI membership and participation. By formalizing these partnerships, PAPSI aims to create a more connected, supported, and empowered science education community across the country.

    2 Objectives of the Program

    • Strengthen collaboration between PAPSI and educational institutions.
    • Support continuous professional development for science teachers.
    • Encourage active participation in PAPSI programs and activities.
    • Recognize schools that actively support science teacher development.
    • Build a stronger science education community through institutional partnerships.

    3 Scope of the Partnership

    The program is open to public and private schools, colleges, universities, international schools, homeschool organizations, learning centers, and other educational institutions in the Philippines with active science educators who participate in PAPSI activities and maintain active PAPSI membership. PAPSI reserves the right to review and approve partnership applications to ensure alignment with the organization's mission and professional standards.

    5 Definitions

    TermDefinition
    Active PAPSI MembershipA paid and current PAPSI membership for the applicable calendar or academic year.
    Partner School CoordinatorA designated contact person within the partner school responsible for PAPSI-related communications and coordination.
    Group RegistrationA collective registration of two or more active PAPSI-member teachers from the same institution for a single PAPSI event or program.
    Partnership CategoryThe tier of institutional partnership determined by the number of active PAPSI members in the school.
    Annual ReviewThe yearly evaluation of a partner school's active membership count and participation conducted every January.

    6 Requirements for Partnership

    1. Schools and educational institutions applying for partnership with PAPSI must accomplish the official PAPSI Partner Schools Application Form and undergo evaluation and approval by PAPSI.
    2. Partner schools must maintain the required number of active PAPSI members corresponding to their designated partnership category.
    3. Teachers included in the partnership membership count must possess active and fully paid PAPSI membership for the current membership year.
    4. Partner schools are required to designate a Partner School Coordinator who will serve as the official representative for PAPSI-related communications, announcements, and coordination.
    5. Partner schools are encouraged to actively participate in PAPSI seminars, webinars, workshops, conferences, and other educational initiatives throughout the partnership period.

    7 Privileges of PAPSI Partner Schools

    1. Priority Training Announcements

    Partner schools will receive priority messages and early announcements regarding upcoming PAPSI seminars, webinars, workshops, conferences, and training programs.

    2. School Recognition on the PAPSI Website

    The school name and partnership category will be officially listed on the PAPSI website as a recognized PAPSI Partner School within 30 days of approved partnership.

    3. Priority Access to Limited-Slot Trainings

    Partner schools may receive priority consideration for trainings, workshops, or special events with limited participant slots.

    4. Eligibility for Exclusive Partner-School Activities

    Partner schools may be invited to selected exclusive activities, pilot programs, consultations, or collaborative educational initiatives organized by PAPSI.

    5. Digital Recognition Certificate

    Partner schools shall receive a digital certificate or recognition badge acknowledging their active partnership and support for science teacher professional development, issued within 30 days of approved partnership.

    6. Feature Opportunities

    Selected partner schools shall be featured on PAPSI social media pages or the PAPSI website for their active participation in science education initiatives and teacher development programs.

    7. Streamlined School-Based Coordination

    Partner schools may coordinate institutional or group participation more efficiently through designated communication channels and consolidated registration processes.

    8. Access to Future Collaborative Programs

    Partner schools may be considered for future collaborative projects, teacher development initiatives, research activities, or educational partnerships organized by PAPSI.

    Note: Specific privileges may vary by partnership category. Higher-tier partner schools (Distinguished and Premier) may be given additional priority consideration for exclusive activities, feature opportunities, and future collaborative programs.

    8 Responsibilities of Partner Schools

    1. Promote Professional Development Participation
      Encourage science educators within the institution to participate in PAPSI professional development programs and educational initiatives.
    2. Support Ethical and Professional Conduct
      Uphold professionalism and ethical conduct in all PAPSI-related activities and engagements.
    3. Participate in Partnership Renewal Review
      Cooperate with PAPSI during annual partnership evaluation and renewal processes.
    4. Share Relevant PAPSI Opportunities
      Assist in disseminating relevant PAPSI opportunities, announcements, and activities to science educators within the institution.
    5. Strengthen Institutional Collaboration
      Foster a collaborative relationship with PAPSI in support of science education, teacher development, and academic engagement.
    Note: Partnership categories are reviewed annually based on active membership and institutional participation in PAPSI programs and activities. Schools that no longer meet the requirements may be subject to partnership category reassessment or partnership status review.

    9 Responsibilities of PAPSI

    • Provide timely updates and announcements regarding PAPSI activities and programs.
    • Organize relevant and high-quality professional development programs for science educators.
    • Recognize and acknowledge partner schools appropriately through official channels.
    • Maintain open communication and coordination with partner institutions.
    • Publish an annual program summary or report of PAPSI activities and partnerships to promote transparency and accountability.

    10 Application and Renewal Process

    Schools may apply for partnership through the official PAPSI application process by submitting the required information regarding active PAPSI members within the institution. The application form is available on the PAPSI website at papsicenter.com.

    Partnership status is reviewed and renewed annually every January. Schools must reconfirm their active membership count to maintain or upgrade their partnership category. Schools that fall below the minimum membership threshold during the year will be notified and given an opportunity to meet the requirement before the annual review. Failure to meet the requirement at the time of renewal will result in a downgrade to the appropriate category or removal from the program.

    11 Recognition and Public Listing

    Partner schools will be recognized through the following channels within 30 days of approved partnership or renewal:

    • Official listing on the PAPSI website (papsicenter.com)
    • Digital recognition certificate or partner-school badge
    • Social media acknowledgments on official PAPSI pages
    • Inclusion in official PAPSI announcements and communications

    12 Future Opportunities

    PAPSI is committed to expanding the Partner Schools Program to provide greater value to institutional partners. Future directions under active consideration include:

    • School-Based Training Programs — Bringing PAPSI-organized trainings and workshops directly to partner school campuses.
    • Curriculum Consultation Services — Offering guidance and resources to support science curriculum planning and development.
    • Collaborative Research Initiatives — Engaging partner schools in joint research activities, publications, and educational studies.

    Additional opportunities and benefits may be introduced as the program grows and evolves.

    A Annex A: Frequently Asked Questions

    Q1: Who can apply for the PAPSI Partner Schools Program?
    Any public or private school, college, university, or educational institution in the Philippines with at least five (5) active PAPSI-member science educators may apply.
    Q2: How do we apply?
    Visit the PAPSI website at papsicenter.com and complete the official partner school application form. Provide the names and membership details of active PAPSI members within your institution.
    Q3: What counts as active PAPSI membership?
    A teacher is considered an active member if they have a paid and current PAPSI membership for the applicable year.
    Q4: Can our school be upgraded to a higher partnership category?
    Yes. Schools that increase their number of active PAPSI members to meet the threshold of a higher category may apply for an upgrade during the annual review period every January.
    Q5: What happens if our school falls below the required number of members?
    PAPSI will notify the school and provide an opportunity to meet the requirement. If the school is unable to meet the threshold by the annual review, the partnership category will be adjusted accordingly or the partnership may be placed on hold.
    Q6: Are the privileges the same for all partnership categories?
    Core privileges apply to all partner schools. However, higher-tier partners (Distinguished and Premier) may receive additional priority consideration for exclusive activities, feature opportunities, and collaborative programs.
    Q7: How will we receive our digital recognition certificate?
    The digital certificate will be sent to the designated Partner School Coordinator via official PAPSI communication channels within 30 days of approved partnership.
    Q8: Is there a fee to join the PAPSI Partner Schools Program?
    There is no separate fee for the partnership program itself. However, individual teacher members must maintain active and paid PAPSI membership to be counted toward the school's partnership category.
